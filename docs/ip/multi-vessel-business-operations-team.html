Multi-Vessel Business Operations Team

Summary

This draft establishes prior art around role-specific AI workers coordinating around business workflows. It does not reveal client playbooks, private routing doctrine, real workflows, internal bridge details, production memory, or vessel soul design.

Thesis

A business does not need one generic chatbot. A business needs a coordinated team of role-specific AI workers.

Each worker can own a domain, maintain relevant context, use appropriate tools, and escalate to humans when judgment or approval is required.

Core Pattern

A multi-vessel business operations team includes:

  1. Role-specific agents — each agent has a defined operational domain.
  2. Shared operating context — agents can work from approved business documents, tasks, and status.
  3. Human approval gates — sensitive decisions remain supervised.
  4. Task routing — work is routed to the right role rather than handled by one generic assistant.
  5. Continuity — agents preserve current work state across interruptions.
  6. Auditability — actions and decisions can be reviewed.

Why This Matters

Real businesses are not single-threaded. Operations include finance, customer support, sales, scheduling, procurement, reporting, and internal coordination.

A single assistant becomes overloaded. A role-specific team can be clearer, safer, and easier to govern.

Defensive Claim

The category established here is:

A coordinated team of role-specific AI workers for business operations, using task routing, shared approved context, continuity, auditability, and human approval gates.

Boundaries

  • No real client workflows.
  • No private operating playbooks.
  • No internal routing doctrine.
  • No private coordination internals.
  • No production deployment details.
  • No real memory or identity doctrine.
  • No customer data.
  • No pricing or packaging strategy.

Public Example

A safe toy example:

A sample company uses three fake agents: one summarizes inbound requests, one drafts task updates, and one prepares weekly status reports. All external sends require human approval.

Closing

The next generation of business automation will look less like a chatbot and more like an operating team: specialized, supervised, continuous, and auditable.
